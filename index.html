About Me

I'm a passionate computer science student at Dublin High School, deeply interested in the intersection of robotics and artificial intelligence. My journey in technology began with VEX Robotics, which sparked my interest in swarm robotics and AI/ML research.

As the team captain of Dublin High School's VEX Robotics team, I've led our group to multiple regional championships and developed innovative solutions for complex engineering challenges. My experience in competitive robotics has taught me valuable lessons in leadership, problem-solving, and the importance of iterative design. Through countless hours of programming and mechanical design, I've gained hands-on experience with sensors, actuators, and real-time control systems.

My fascination with swarm robotics emerged from observing natural phenomena like ant colonies and bird flocks. This inspired me to explore how multiple robots can work together to solve complex tasks. Currently, I'm working on developing algorithms for distributed robot coordination and studying their applications in search and rescue operations. I'm also conducting independent research in machine learning, focusing on reinforcement learning techniques for robotic navigation and decision-making.
